The Health Risks of Carcass and Droppings might be more serious than you think. Contamination of water supplies by unburied bodies, burial sites, or temporary storage sites may result in the spread of gastroenteritis from normal intestinal contents. To those in close contact with the dead, such as rescue workers, there is a health risk from chronic infectious diseases which those killed may have been suffering from and which spread by direct contact, including hepatitis B and hepatitis C, HIV, enteric intestinal pathogens, tuberculosis, cholera and others

Animal Dropping are responsible for spreading diseases like Campylobacteriosis, E.Coli, Roundworm, Tapeworm, Diarrhea, Dysentery etc. There are over 100 different varieties of viruses, bacteria, and helminthes in droppings. All come from droppings that are untreated or have not been properly treated. Viruses cannot multiply outside of a host, but they can survive for several weeks, depending on temperature, while waiting for a new host. The concern is what happens when the bacteria leave the body in feces and are then ingested. The bacteria that we are most concerned with are E. coli, Salmonella, Shigella, and Vibrio. These are the bacteria that cause the main ill health effects in humans.

Why you need Carcass and Dropping Removal?


When animals die or are slaughtered on farms, carcasses and parts that are unfit for use as food should be disposed of properly. Safe and environmentally responsible disposal of animal carcasses, whether an individual death or during significant mortality events, is an essential consideration. Dead animals can cause all sorts of health issues due to the parasites that are contained in various animal carcasses. Not to mention all of the insects and animals that typically gather around and feed on carcasses.

If the body is allowed to sit there for long enough without getting properly sanitized, it can even contaminate the surrounding soil or water with parasites that can cause health issues like cryptosporidium and bayliscaris. That is why if you have a dead critter in your property, it is important to get it cleaned up right away by an expert like us.

Bird droppings or guano can not only pose a high health risk, but they also damage building structures and make building look unsightly. If not dealt with immediately, guano can cause permanent damage to balconies, roofs, fences, machinery, gardening equipment and ventilation systems. These can all be very expensive to replace so it’s very important that a professional cleaning service is hired to properly remove and disinfect affected areas. Although bird droppings can seem fairly harmless, it is quite the opposite. Guano is highly acidic and in turn can cause corrosion of surfaces if left unremoved for medium to long periods of time. Not only that but bat guano in particular can be dangerous to humans and animals as it carries rabies and can cause diseases such as histoplasmosis which is a type of lung infection.
