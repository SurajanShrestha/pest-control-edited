Effects of Carcass and Dropping at Your Home/Office

Carcass and Droppings might be more serious than you think. Contamination of water supplies by unburied bodies/carcasses may result in the spread of gastroenteritis from the dead body's intestinal parts. People in close contact with the dead might have health risks from chronic infectious diseases which those killed may have been suffering from and which spread by direct contact, including hepatitis B and hepatitis C, HIV, enteric intestinal pathogens, tuberculosis, cholera and others

Animal Droppings are not safe either. There are over 100 different varieties of viruses and bacteria in untreated droppings which are responsible for spreading diseases like Campylobacteriosis, E.Coli, Roundworm, Tapeworm, Diarrhea, Dysentery etc. Viruses cannot multiply outside of a host, but they can survive for several weeks, depending on temperature, while waiting for a new host. When the bacteria leaves the body in feces and are then ingested by humans, it becomes a major problem to look at. The bacteria that we are most concerned with are E. coli, Salmonella, Shigella, and Vibrio. These are the bacteria that cause the main ill health effects in humans.

Why you need Carcass and Dropping Removal?


When animals die or are slaughtered on farms, carcasses and parts that are unfit for use as food should be disposed of properly. Safe and environmentally responsible disposal of animal carcasses, whether an individual death or during significant mortality events, is an essential consideration. Dead animals can cause all sorts of health issues due to the parasites that are contained in various animal carcasses. Not to mention all of the insects and animals that typically gather around and feed on carcasses.

If the body is allowed to sit there for long enough without getting properly sanitized, it can even contaminate the surrounding soil or water with parasites that can cause health issues like cryptosporidium and bayliscaris. That is why if you have a dead critter in your property, it is important to get it cleaned up right away by an expert like us.

Bird droppings can not only pose a high health risk, but they also damage building structures and make building look unsightly. If not dealt with immediately, dropping can cause permanent damage to balconies, roofs, fences, machinery, gardening equipment and ventilation systems. These can all be very expensive to replace so it’s very important that a professional cleaning service is hired to properly remove and disinfect affected areas. Although bird droppings can seem fairly harmless, it is quite the opposite. Dropping is highly acidic and in turn can cause corrosion of surfaces if left unremoved for medium to long periods of time. Not only that but bat dropping in particular can be dangerous to humans and animals as it carries rabies and can cause diseases such as histoplasmosis which is a type of lung infection.
