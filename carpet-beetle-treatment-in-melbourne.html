Effects of Carpet Beetle at Your Home/Office

Are Carpet Beetles harmful? The short answer is, yes, carpet beetles can be harmful. These insects don't bite humans, but they can cause a bumpy, itchy, rash that is sometimes mistaken for bed bug bites. Airborne fibers from carpet beetles can also cause respiratory tract and eye irritation. However, it often takes years for most people to become sensitized to these fibers.

Carpet Beetle also spread Dermatitis. Some symptoms include intense itching and rash. Additionally, in some patients, irritation of the respiratory tract and eyes may develop. They also cause Gastrointestinal Canthariasis. If carpet beetles get in contact with the foods left open, because of the saliva and feces they left on these foods, they pose a threat to human health as well as causing foods to go bad and infected with bacteria. When those bacteria enter the human body, they can cause infections and several serious diseases.

Adult carpet beetles can fly, which makes them adept at getting into our homes. And since these are overwintering pests, they are motivated to get in every year. You won't typically find these creatures feeding on synthetic fabrics unless they have perspiration, oil, or foods soaked into them.
